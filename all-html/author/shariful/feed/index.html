eWages Ecosystem for Migrant Workers in Sabah

In recent years, the need for reliable, secure, and accessible financial solutions for migrant workers in Sabah has grown significantly. The eWages ecosystem, developed and supported by licensed financial technology (fintech) providers such as MobilityOne and TFP Solutions Berhad, offers a comprehensive platform designed to meet these needs. By leveraging modern financial tools and regulatory approvals, this system provides essential services to foreign workers, ensuring secure access to their wages and enabling financial inclusion.
 
  1. MobilityOne (M1): A pivotal fintech entity listed on the AIM of the London Stock Exchange (LON: MBO), MobilityOne plays a critical role in offering financial services, including eWallet and prepaid Mastercard solutions. With a license from Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM) as an operator and acquirer, MobilityOne ensures that payments are processed efficiently and securely. The company’s wallet system has received approval from the Ministry of Human Resources (Jabatan Tenaga Kerja Semenanjung Malaysia – JTKSM), further cementing its credibility as a reliable service provider for wage distribution.        
  2. TFP Solutions Berhad: Listed on the Bursa Malaysia ACE Market (KLSE: TFP 0145), TFP Solutions Berhad is renowned for its IT and hardware services, as well as HR and payroll solutions. With over 12 years of experience, TFP has provided innovative solutions for various sectors and is licensed by the Housing and Local Government Ministry (KPKT) to offer conventional loans. Their collaboration with MobilityOne ensures a seamless integration of wage disbursement with robust digital solutions for workers.
 
The eWages ecosystem is designed to offer various services that directly benefit migrant workers, including:
 
  • eWallet and Prepaid Mastercard Access: Migrant workers can receive their salaries directly into their eWallets or linked prepaid Mastercards, allowing them easy, cashless access to their funds.
  • ATM Cash Withdrawals: For workers preferring physical cash, eWages allows withdrawals from ATMs, ensuring convenience and ease of use.
  • Mobile Payments and QR Transactions: The ecosystem supports mobile SIM payments and QR code transactions for everyday expenses such as groceries.
  • Remittance Services: Workers can send money back to their home countries through a licensed remittance app, simplifying cross-border financial transfers.
 
A critical aspect of the eWages system is its adherence to stringent regulatory requirements. MobilityOne’s licenses from BNM, including its status as a trusted Mastercard issuer and an approved merchant acquirer, guarantee secure, compliant financial operations. The Ministry of Human Resources’ (JTKSM) approval of the wallet system highlights the ecosystem’s commitment to meeting the legal standards for salary distribution to foreign workers.
 
Opening a salary card account within this ecosystem is straightforward and efficient. Migrant workers only need to present a valid passport to set up an account, making financial services accessible without burdensome documentation requirements. This simple yet secure onboarding process ensures that more workers can join the eWages ecosystem and benefit from its range of services.
 
The eWages ecosystem represents a transformative step in supporting the financial needs of migrant workers in Sabah. Through the partnership between MobilityOne and TFP Solutions Berhad, workers can access modern financial tools that enhance their financial autonomy, improve security, and facilitate smooth transactions. This comprehensive solution not only improves the quality of life for foreign workers but also contributes to the broader financial inclusion objectives in Malaysia.
